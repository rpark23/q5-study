# Lecture 06: PTSD

## Diagnosis
- Symptoms must be present for **more than one month**

---

## Symptoms Targeted by Pharmacotherapy

1. **Intrusion**
2. **Avoidance**
3. **Changes in mood + cognition**
4. **Changes in arousal + reactivity**

---

## Approved Medications for PTSD

| Drug | Class |
|------|-------|
| **Paroxetine** | SSRI |
| **Sertraline** | SSRI |

### Paroxetine Additional Indications (not shared with Sertraline)
- Generalized anxiety disorder
- Vasomotor symptoms of menopause

---

## Medications to AVOID in PTSD

| Medication | Reason to Avoid |
|------------|-----------------|
| **Benzodiazepines** | Addiction risk, memory issues |
| **Z-class sedatives** | Limited evidence for efficacy in PTSD insomnia |
| **Cannabinoids** | Not studied enough yet |

---

## Summary

| Topic | Key Point |
|-------|-----------|
| Diagnosis duration | > 1 month of symptoms |
| Approved drugs | Paroxetine, Sertraline (both SSRIs) |
| Avoid | Benzos, Z-drugs, cannabinoids |
| Target symptoms | Intrusion, avoidance, mood/cognition changes, arousal/reactivity changes |
