# Lecture 09: General Anesthetics

## Goals of General Anesthesia (5 Primary Effects)

1. **Amnesia** - No memory of procedure
2. **Unconsciousness** - Loss of awareness
3. **Immobility** - No movement to surgical stimulation
4. **Analgesia** - Pain relief
5. **Autonomic stability** - Controlled hemodynamics

---

## Theoretical Framework

- Traditional theory: Disruption of lipid membrane bilayer
- Modern: Multiple molecular targets including GABA-A receptors, NMDA receptors, and ion channels
- **EEG changes**: Progressive increase in low-frequency, high-amplitude activity

---

## MAC (Minimum Alveolar Concentration)

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **MAC** | ED50 - % anesthetic gas in alveoli where 50% of patients don't respond to surgical incision |
| **1.2 MAC** | ED95 (2 standard deviations) - adequate for 95% of patients |

### MAC Properties
- **Additive**: Combining anesthetics (e.g., 0.5 MAC of each = 1 MAC total)
- Lower MAC = more potent anesthetic

### Factors Affecting MAC

| Factor | Effect on MAC |
|--------|--------------|
| Increasing age | Decreases MAC |
| Hypothermia | Decreases MAC |
| Opioids | Decreases MAC |
| Hypoxia | Decreases MAC |

---

## Pharmacokinetics of Volatile Anesthetics

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Inspiratory concentration** | More given = more diffuses |
| **Blood:gas partition coefficient (solubility)** | Low solubility = faster onset AND offset |
| **Ventilation rate** | Increasing ventilation enhances induction more for **soluble** anesthetics (halothane) |

---

## Volatile (Inhaled) Anesthetics

### Top 5 Inhaled Anesthetics
1. Sevoflurane
2. Isoflurane
3. Desflurane
4. Nitrous oxide (N2O)
5. Halothane (rarely used)

### Systemic Effects

| System | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Respiratory** | Decrease ventilatory drive |
| **Cardiovascular** | Decrease BP via decreased peripheral vascular resistance |
| **Cerebral** | Increase cerebral blood flow, increase intracranial pressure |
| **GI** | Increase nausea and vomiting |

### Environmental Note
- **Desflurane** is the worst for the environment (potent greenhouse gas)

---

## Toxicities of Volatile Anesthetics

1. **Malignant hyperthermia** - all except N2O
2. **Renal toxicity**
3. **Mutagenic, teratogenic** - risk of miscarriages, neuronal death

*Requires scavenging of gas from anesthesia machine*

## IV Anesthetics

| Drug | Class | Key Points |
|------|-------|------------|
| **Midazolam** | Benzodiazepine | Prototypical IV anesthetic |
| **Fentanyl** | Opioid | Prototypical IV opioid |
| **Propofol** | Hypnotic | Fast onset/offset, can cause **Propofol Infusion Syndrome** |
| **Etomidate** | Hypnotic | No analgesic effects, good hemodynamic stability |
| **Ketamine** | Dissociative | NMDA antagonist, analgesic properties |

---

## Malignant Hyperthermia

### Mechanism
- Genetic defect in **RyR1** (ryanodine receptor)
- Triggered by volatile anesthetics + succinylcholine
- Uncontrolled calcium release from sarcoplasmic reticulum

### Treatment
- **Dantrolene** (blocks RyR1)

---

## Hyperthermic Syndromes Comparison

| Syndrome | Cause |
|----------|-------|
| **Malignant hyperthermia** | Anesthesia (volatile agents, succinylcholine) |
| **Neuroleptic malignant syndrome** | Antipsychotics |
| **Serotonin syndrome** | Antidepressants (SSRIs, MAOIs) |
