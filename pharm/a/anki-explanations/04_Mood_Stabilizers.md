# Lecture 04: Mood Stabilizers

## Overview
- Primarily indicated for **bipolar disorder**, particularly the **manic** phase
- **3 main classes**: Lithium, Anticonvulsant-type, Antipsychotic medications
- **All cause two major side effects**: Teratogenesis and Weight gain

---

## Lithium

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **MOA** | Acts on intracellular secondary messengers (e.g., inositol) |
| **Therapeutic Index** | **LOW** (requires careful monitoring) |
| **Efficacy** | Decreases pure/euphoric mania (1-2 weeks), helps maintenance, reduces suicidal thoughts |

### Side Effects
| Side Effect | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Renal** | Renal failure, SIADH |
| **Thyroid** | **Hypo**thyroidism |
| **Cognitive** | Cognitive slowing |
| **Teratogenic** | **Ebstein anomaly** (malformed tricuspid valve) |

---

## Valproic Acid

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **MOA** | Blocks Na+ channels, increases GABA release |
| **Therapeutic Index** | **HIGH** |
| **Best for** | **Rapid cycling** bipolar disorder |

### Side Effects
| Side Effect | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Hepatic** | **Hepatic failure** |
| **Teratogenic** | Neural tube defects |
| **Hematologic** | Thrombocytopenia |

---

## Carbamazepine (Tegretol)

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **MOA** | Some 5-HT activity (similar to TCA) |
| **Therapeutic Index** | Moderate |
| **Best for** | **Mixed mania** |

### Side Effects
| Side Effect | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Drug interactions** | **CYP450 inducer** (major concern!) |
| **Hematologic** | **Lethal anemia** (aplastic anemia, agranulocytosis) |

---

## Lamotrigine

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Type** | Anticonvulsant-type mood stabilizer |
| **Best for** | Bipolar depression |

### Side Effects
| Side Effect | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Dermatologic** | **Stevens-Johnson syndrome** (severe, life-threatening rash) |

---

## Important Clinical Points

### Antidepressants in Bipolar Disorder
- **NOT usually used!**
- Can destabilize and cause **more rapid cycling**

### Adjunctive Treatment
- **Benzodiazepines** can be used as adjunctive treatment in bipolar disorder

## Non-Pharmacologic Treatments

### Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)
- Brief electrical stimulation of brain under anesthesia
- **80-90% response rate** for major depressive episode

### Repetitive Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation (rTMS)
- Electromagnetic coil delivers magnetic pulse to mood control regions
- Efficacy similar to medication
- Safe and tolerable
- Requires **20-30 treatments**

### Non-Monoamine Antidepressants
- **Ketamine** (NMDA antagonist)
- Experimental: LSD, Psilocybin
