## Key Points

### SSRIs
- First-line treatment for depression
- **Citalopram** benefit: Safer regarding interactions with cytochrome P450 enzymes

### TCAs (Tricyclic Antidepressants)
- Older class, more side effects than SSRIs
- Block reuptake of serotonin and norepinephrine

### MAOIs (Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors)
- Inhibit breakdown of monoamines (serotonin, NE, dopamine)
- Dietary restrictions required (tyramine-containing foods)

### SNRIs
- Dual reuptake inhibition (serotonin + norepinephrine)
- May be helpful for pain conditions

### 5HT-2 Antagonists
- Block serotonin 5HT-2 receptors
- Different mechanism than reuptake inhibitors

## Summary by Mechanism

| Mechanism | Drugs |
|-----------|-------|
| **Serotonin reuptake inhibition** | SSRIs (Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Paroxetine, Citalopram) |
| **Serotonin + NE reuptake inhibition** | SNRIs (Venlafaxine, Duloxetine), TCAs (Imipramine, Amitriptyline) |
| **MAO inhibition** | MAOIs (Phenelzine, Tranylcypromine) |
| **5HT-2 receptor antagonism** | Serzone |
